# Research Round Log

## Round 1: Broad Patterns

### Hypotheses Tested
1. Sentiment varies by site
2. Sentiment varies by position
3. HR attributes (transfer status, building wing, room type, origin system quality) affect sentiment
4. Tenure and age correlate with sentiment

### Results

**Site Differences (Partially Supported)**
- Valley High least positive (+0.095), Riverside most positive (+0.201), Hillcrest middle (+0.166)
- Gap persists within same positions — not explained by staffing mix
- Valley High admins nearly neutral (+0.004) vs elementary admins strongly positive (+0.42-0.47)

**Position Differences (Partially Supported)**
- Food Service only negative group (mean -0.032) — bell schedule disruptions
- District Office most positive (+0.418) — proximity to decision-making
- Custodial staff anomalously negative at Valley High (-0.093) but positive at elementaries
- Site Admins show huge site interaction: very positive at elementaries, neutral at Valley High

**HR Fields (Strongly Supported)**
- Transfers significantly more positive (+0.334 vs +0.119)
- Origin system quality creates 0.48 gap: "worse" origins at +0.582 (86.7% positive, 0% negative), "better" origins at +0.102
- North Wing (Hillcrest only, n=30) is net-negative (-0.004) — likely physical installation issue
- Large/Specialized rooms (Valley High only, n=20) very positive (+0.370)

**Tenure/Age (Partially Supported)**
- District tenure is strongest single predictor (r=-0.24), monotonic decline
- 20+ years is only negative band (-0.113)
- Age shows NO correlation (r=+0.057) — it's institutional attachment, not age
- Operational staff (custodial, food service) show steepest tenure drop-off
- Counselors/Specialists are exception: 20+ years still positive (+0.293)

### Emerging Questions
- Why are Valley High admins/custodial so different from elementary peers?
- Does tenure interact with transfer status?
- What's happening in Hillcrest's 0-3 tenure band (anomalously low)?
- Gender and race_ethnicity patterns not yet examined
- Is the North Wing issue purely physical, or are there demographic confounders?

## Round 2: Interactions & Alternative Explanations

### Hypotheses Tested
1. Valley High's lower sentiment is broad-based, not just custodial/admin
2. Tenure × transfer interaction
3. Gender and race/ethnicity patterns
4. Hillcrest 0-3 anomaly driven by North Wing; North Wing is a physical issue

### Results

**Valley High Deep-Dive (Supported: Broad-Based)**
- 6 of 8 positions below district mean — deficit persists after removing custodial/admin
- Teachers (n=96) are negative: communication failures + system reliability complaints
- Large/Specialized rooms (n=20, +0.370) are the bright spot vs Standard rooms (+0.064)
- Core themes: blindsided by installation, glitches, lost instructional time

**Tenure × Transfer (Partially Supported, with structural caveat)**
- All 68 transfers are 0-5 years at district — cannot test long-tenured transfers
- Transfer boost is modest (+0.12) after matching for tenure
- Origin system quality (+0.48 spread) is the dominant driver — reference frame effect
- Key insight: satisfaction is relative to prior experience, not absolute

**Gender & Race (Partially Supported)**
- Gender gap is real and persistent: Female +0.197 vs Male +0.038
- Males at Valley High are strikingly negative (-0.154) — biggest gender-site interaction
- Exception: males MORE positive at Riverside (+0.253 vs female +0.183)
- Race effects smaller: Asian staff at VH anomalously positive; White 20+ yr staff most negative cell (-0.231)
- Long-tenured Hispanic/Latino staff (+0.253) dramatically above 20+ yr band mean (-0.113)

**Hillcrest North Wing (Strongly Supported: Physical Issue)**
- 100% of North Wing staff mention audio/speaker problems — universal, not demographic
- North Wing demographics are not unusual vs rest of Hillcrest
- Removing 4 North Wing staff from Hillcrest 0-3yr cohort raises sentiment from +0.111 to +0.253
- Paraprofessionals and counselors hit hardest (cite interruptions during sensitive sessions)
- Actionable finding: facilities investigation needed, not change management

### Emerging Questions
- Why are males at Valley High so much more negative? Position composition? Building area?
- What themes emerge from qualitative reading of the most negative responses?
- Do the Q2 (training) and Q3 (communication) responses tell a different story than overall sentiment?
- Is there a question-level pattern — are some sites worse on specific questions?
- What explains the counselor exception (positive even at 20+ years)?

## Round 3: Within-Site Variation & Question-Level Analysis

### Hypotheses Tested
1. Question-level sentiment varies by site — VH weak on Q2/Q3
2. Valley High male negativity explained by position composition
3. Long-tenured counselors stay positive due to safety/emergency features
4. Sites differ in polarization (variance)

### Results

**Question-Level Analysis (Partially Refuted — Surprise)**
- Valley High's weakness is Q1 (experience) and Q5 (open feedback), NOT Q2/Q3
- Valley High actually LEADS on Q2 (training: +0.200)
- Q4 (system functionality) is universally positive — staff like the product, dislike the process
- Q5 is negative across ALL sites — open-ended prompts surface latent frustration
- Site Admins positive overall but negative on Q5 — seeing problems they don't voice in structured Qs

**Valley High Male Negativity (Refuted — Not Position-Driven)**
- Gender gap persists after controlling for position (-0.339 raw → -0.319 controlled)
- Male teachers at VH: -0.204 vs female teachers: +0.160 (n=18 vs 76)
- Males more negative in 6 of 8 positions — broad-based, not custodial artifact
- Male teachers more often question the necessity of the change itself

**Counselor Exception (Supported)**
- 92% of long-tenured counselors mention safety/emergency features (vs 25% of teachers)
- All 13 long-tenured counselors mention disruption too — but weight safety higher
- Professional lens: counselors evaluate through safety frame, teachers through workflow frame
- Pattern holds across all three sites, strongest at Riverside

**Within-Site Variance (Refuted — Opposite of Expected)**
- Riverside is MOST polarized (SD=0.605), not Valley High (0.530)
- ALL 10 most negative records are from Riverside
- ALL 10 most positive records are from Valley High
- Riverside's high average masks deep internal divisions
- Custodial staff most polarized subgroup at every site

### Emerging Questions
- What drives Riverside's extreme polarization? Why do they have both the happiest and angriest staff?
- What are the specific themes in the 10 most negative Riverside responses?
- Is there a "silent middle" — staff giving bland neutral responses that mask real opinions?
- Do gender patterns at Riverside (where males are MORE positive) tell us something about the VH effect?

## Round 4: Qualitative Deep-Dive

### Hypotheses Tested
1. What separates the most negative from most positive Riverside staff?
2. What communication failure themes appear in Q3, and who experienced them?

### Results

**Riverside Polarization (Clear Finding)**
- The single biggest differentiator: whether the system reliably works for that person
- 6/10 most negative staff center complaints on intercom failures, cutouts, garbled audio
- Most positive staff report a system that "just works" plus favorable reference frames
- Secondary factors: transfer origin, tenure/age, training quality perception
- Even positive staff have complaints — but their core daily experience works

**Q3 Communication Themes (Strongly Supported: Hierarchical Failure)**
- Communication failure followed the organizational hierarchy
- 0% of custodial and food service staff used positive communication language
- Paraprofessionals' top complaint: "surprised" (6.5%) — "Nobody told the aides anything"
- 11-20 year staff had double the complaint rate of new staff (18.9% vs 9.3%)
- Dominant themes: lack of transparency (4.6%), blindsided (3.4%), surprised (2.6%)
- Teachers had the widest spread: 18% negative themes but also 13.7% positive

## Round 5: Challenge Findings

### Hypotheses Tested
1. Is the sentiment scorer systematically biased?
2. Is the tenure correlation driven by operational staff?

### Results

**Scorer Validity (Findings Hold Up)**
- 95% agreement between auto and context-aware scoring on random sample
- No key finding is a scorer artifact — site rankings, transfer gap, tenure gradient all preserved
- Transfer scores are actually UNDERSTATED (the "worse" keyword penalizes positive comparisons)
- Blind spots: negation (26.8% of records), hedging (13%), length extremes
- Hedging records (13%) average -0.002 vs +0.171 for non-hedging — a "silent dissatisfaction" signal

**Tenure Robustness (Refuted: Tenure Effect is Robust)**
- Removing Food Service + Custodial: r drops from -0.24 to -0.23 (negligible change)
- Teachers alone: r=-0.17, p=0.008 — still significant
- ALL 8 positions show negative tenure-sentiment correlation
- District tenure, NOT career length, is what matters (partial r for years_in_profession = 0.01)
- Operational roles amplify the pattern (~2x stronger) but don't create it

### Emerging Questions
- Which demographics use hedging language most? Could reveal "silent dissatisfaction"
- Why does Riverside's gender pattern reverse (males more positive)?
- Are there any unexplored interaction effects?

## Round 6: Final Synthesis — Edge Cases, Masking, and Narrative Check

### Hypotheses Tested
1. Who are the "hedgers" — staff using resigned language scored as neutral?
2. Do variables that look flat in aggregate mask opposite effects in subgroups?
3. Do qualitative readings confirm quantitative findings? Any missed themes?

### Results

**Hedging & Silent Dissatisfaction (Supported)**
- 88 respondents (17.6%) use hedging language ("it's fine", "I guess", "good enough")
- Custodial (30.3%) and Food Service (28.9%) hedge at nearly 2x baseline
- Mid-tenure (11-20 years) have highest rate (22%); new staff and admins almost never hedge
- Reclassifying hedgers shifts district negative rate from 28.8% to 34.2%
- These are "quiet dissenters" — acceptance without endorsement

**Masking Effects (Strong Findings)**
- Race differences explode among long-tenured staff (3-4x larger than among newcomers)
  - Hispanic/Latino 20+ yr: +0.253 vs White 20+ yr: -0.231
- Gender effect flips by site: males +0.070 at Riverside, -0.339 at Valley High
- Male custodial staff: +0.800 at Riverside, -0.497 at Valley High — 1.3-point swing
- Variables flat in aggregate hide the most dramatic subgroup differences

**Narrative Coherence (All Findings Confirmed + New Themes)**
- Tenure effect, site gaps, transfer positivity, North Wing issue all visible in individual stories
- New themes discovered:
  1. "Compliance without consent" — staff want to be stakeholders, not just implementers
  2. Instructional time loss — Valley High teachers frame system as costing student learning
  3. Counselor confidentiality — intercom interruptions during sensitive student sessions
  4. Phased rollout criticism — experienced staff recommend gradual deployment
- Numbers flatten emotional register — a score of -0.3 can mean genuine harm to students
- Site admins speak in "official voice" even in anonymous surveys — likely less candid
